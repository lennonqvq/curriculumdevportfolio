Reflection

After finishing the first unit of Training Methodology II of TESDA, I have realized the importance of understanding or knowing our target learners and the analysis of training regulations—these two are the foundation of constructing a curriculum. Profiling learners is crucial. Crucial enough that even if you have the most robust curriculum, but if you don't account for your learner's prior points of view, educational background, or learning styles, everything will fail. With this, it will allow you, as the trainer, to gap between their current skill level or prior knowledge to the curriculum's requirements. Understanding training regulations on the other hand ensures us, the trainers, that every module we build aligns with what the actual workplace needs. Without understanding it, our instruction, as trainer, will lack connection to the outside world. Knowing these two ensures that the curriculum is both relevant and learner-centered.
